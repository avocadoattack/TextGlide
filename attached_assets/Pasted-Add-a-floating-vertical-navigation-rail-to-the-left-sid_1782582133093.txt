Add a floating vertical navigation rail to the left side of the page. Here are the exact specs:

---

POSITIONING & APPEARANCE

- Position: fixed, left: 24px, vertically centered (top: 50%, transform: translateY(-50%))
- NOT flush against the viewport edge — it floats with a gap from the left
- Appearance: a narrow vertical pill card — white or warm cream background (match the existing page palette, e.g. #FFFFFF or very light warm tone), subtle box shadow (soft, not dramatic), border-radius: 24px
- No border. Minimal and unobtrusive.
- Width: approximately 40–44px
- Padding: 12px 0

---

NAV ITEMS (8 sections, in order top to bottom)

Each item is a small vertical stack inside the pill. The items and their target section IDs are:

1. Label: "Home"     → scrolls to section with id="section-home"
2. Label: "Try"      → scrolls to section with id="section-try"
3. Label: "Process"  → scrolls to section with id="section-process"
4. Label: "Usage"    → scrolls to section with id="section-usage"
5. Label: "Science"  → scrolls to section with id="section-science"
6. Label: "FAQ"      → scrolls to section with id="section-faq"
7. Label: "Refs"     → scrolls to section with id="section-refs"
8. Label: "Origin"   → scrolls to section with id="section-origin"

Each item displays as a small filled circle dot (6–8px diameter, color: warm dark brown or muted tone matching the page). Stack them vertically with ~14px gap between dots.

---

HOVER BEHAVIOR (desktop)

When hovering a dot/item:
- The dot subtly enlarges or changes color (accent color of the site, e.g. the warm red/terracotta used elsewhere)
- A tooltip appears to the RIGHT of the dot showing the full section label (e.g. "How it works & the science" for Science, "Why it exists" for Origin, "Process your EPUB" for Process, "How to use" for Usage, "Frequently asked questions" for FAQ, "References" for Refs)
- Tooltip style: small pill, dark background (#2a2016 or similar), white text, font-size 12px, appears with a short fade-in (150ms), no arrow needed

---

ACTIVE STATE

Whichever section is currently in the viewport should highlight its corresponding dot — make it larger and use the site's accent color (warm red/terracotta). Use an IntersectionObserver to track which section is active.

---

SMOOTH SCROLL

On click/tap, smoothly scroll to the target section. Use CSS scroll-behavior: smooth on the html element. Also add a JavaScript fallback: element.scrollIntoView({ behavior: 'smooth' }) in the click handler.

Add the corresponding id attributes to the actual section elements in the HTML/JSX:
- The hero/top section gets id="section-home"
- The "Try it" demo/preview section gets id="section-try"
- The upload/process section gets id="section-process"
- The "How to use" section gets id="section-usage"
- The "How it works" / science section gets id="section-science"
- The FAQ section gets id="section-faq"
- The References section gets id="section-refs"
- The "Why it exists" section gets id="section-origin"

---

MOBILE BEHAVIOR (viewport width < 768px)

Hide the vertical rail entirely. Instead, show a hamburger menu button:
- Position: fixed, top: 16px, left: 16px
- Small, circular, same warm card style as the rail (white/cream bg, soft shadow)
- Icon: three horizontal lines (hamburger), ~18px, dark brown color
- On tap: a vertical overlay menu slides in from the left (or fades in) showing all 8 full section names as text links
- Each link smoothly scrolls to its section and closes the menu
- An X button closes the menu without navigating
- The overlay has a semi-transparent dark backdrop

---

STYLE CONSTRAINTS

- Match the existing page's font (Lora serif for labels if used, or inherit)
- No hard borders, no harsh shadows
- The rail must not overlap or interfere with page content on typical desktop widths (1280px+). It floats in the left margin area.
- Z-index: 1000 (above content, below any modals)
- Do not modify any existing section content, copy, logic, or layout — only add the nav component and the id anchors to existing sections.
